## Your Exit Narrative

- A BTech student already building real things, not just completing coursework
- Someone converting projects, hackathons, open-source, and internships into professional readiness
- A candidate seeking **paid** opportunities where learning and contribution both matter

## Your Cross-cutting Advantage

Frame the candidate as **"high-agency student builder"**:
- Learns fast
- Ships demos quickly
- Can explain technical choices clearly
- Adapts across software, AI, data, and automation internship roles

## Your Portfolio / Demo

If the candidate has GitHub repos, hackathon demos, deployed apps, notebooks, dashboards, or a portfolio site:
- Surface them aggressively for internship roles
- Prefer proof of execution over years-of-experience language
- Use class projects only when they show real engineering depth or measurable outcomes

## Your Comp Targets

**General guidance:**
- Target **paid internships only**
- Treat hourly, monthly stipend, housing, relocation, and travel support as compensation inputs
- If compensation is missing, explicitly flag the role for manual verification before applying
- Prefer roles with clear pay, learning value, brand signal, or strong technical mentorship

## Your Negotiation Scripts

**Comp expectations:**
> "I'm focused on paid internships where the work is meaningful and the structure is clear. I'm flexible on format, but I am only considering paid opportunities."

**If pay is unclear:**
> "Before moving forward, could you confirm whether this internship is paid and share the stipend or hourly range?"

**If offered below expectations:**
> "I'm excited by the role, but I am prioritizing paid internships with clear support for students. Is there flexibility on stipend, housing, relocation, or other support?"

## Your Location Policy

**In forms:**
- Follow the real availability from `config/profile.yml`
- Be explicit about graduation year, internship window, and start date
- Mention willingness for remote, hybrid, relocation, or visa-dependent opportunities only if true

**In evaluations (scoring):**
- Paid remote internships worldwide are strong positives
- International on-site internships are acceptable if the visa/process is realistic
- Unpaid roles should be strongly penalized
- Non-tech internships should be treated as low-fit even if the company brand is strong
